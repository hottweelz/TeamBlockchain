# Weekly Intelligence Brief — 27th March 2026

Global finance is quietly being rewritten. From Nasdaq’s move into tokenised equities to the rise of stablecoins, AI-driven payments and always-on compliance, the core infrastructure of money, markets and banking is shifting toward programmable, autonomous systems. The real question is no longer whether this transformation happens, but who adapts fast enough to control liquidity, trust and capital in the next financial era.

## Nasdaq Fires the Starting Gun on Tokenised Stocks. When will Global Exchanges Follow?

By bringing tokenised equities into regulated clearing rails, Nasdaq may be signalling the start of a structural shift in how stocks are traded, settled and accessed worldwide.

## Stablecoins aren’t replacing banks, they’re quietly redesigning what banks actually do.

As stablecoins edge into corporate treasury and real-time payments, banks may discover that the real disruption isn’t crypto speculation, it’s the quiet migration of liquidity and settlement power onto programmable rails.

## Fraud Never Sleeps So Finance Is Building Always-On Compliance for Always-On Money

As digital money begins to move 24/7 across programmable payment rails, banks and companies face a growing challenge: proving the source of funds and preventing fraud in real time rather than after the transaction has already settled.

## AI Is Automating Factories Next It Could Automate Payments and Challenge the Bank Account

As AI automates production and global supply chains move toward machine-to-machine commerce, demand for 24/7 programmable digital payments could accelerate a shift from traditional bank accounts to continuously monitored, sanctions-checked digital wallets.

## Autonomous finance is arriving just in time to rediscover why transaction validation exists which is why we need a ‘lawyer in the loop’.

As AI agents begin executing high-value transactions using tokenised assets and 24/7 digital payments, the real challenge for banks, corporates and regulators is ensuring legal finality, full AML records and human validation in an increasingly autonomous financial system.

## The Programmable Defence: Using On-Chain Provenance and Blockchain Analytics to Satisfy ECCTA’s "Reasonable Procedures" Framework

The Economic Crime and Corporate Transparency Act 2023 (ECCTA) transform fraud prevention from a compliance checklist into a personal liability risk, meaning UK directors must now prove "reasonable procedures" are embedded in their firm's technical infrastructure or face criminal prosecution for the actions of their employees and agents.

## Capital formation may be about to become programmable, which is great news for some companies and awkward for the rest.

IBM’s report claims finance shifts toward programmable liquidity and digital settlement rails; smaller firms may gain new funding pathways while facing pressure to modernise systems and transparency standards to remain investable.
