**Weekly selection of articles 6^th^ March 2026**

1.  **[The Banker Warns: Agentic AI Could Bypass Banks
    Entirely](https://www.thebanker.com/content/c30abc03-7b3f-4fca-a683-85430ce9a260).**
    The UK\'s exploration of regulating agentic AI in payments, as
    reported in The Banker signals a profound shift in financial markets
    and advisory practices. Agentic AI, autonomous systems that plan,
    execute, and adapt financial transactions independently, promises
    transformative efficiency: instant, conditional payments, automated
    treasury optimisation, fraud detection, and personalsed investment
    routing. In markets, this could accelerate settlement times, reduce
    intermediaries, and unlock programmable liquidity, boosting trading
    volumes and lowering costs in FX, securities, and cross-border
    flows. Global card networks and European banks already test it live,
    hinting at faster innovation cycles that challenge legacy rails. For
    financial advisors and regulated companies, the impact is
    dual-edged. On one hand, agentic tools enhance due diligence,
    scenario modelling, and client-specific strategies, enabling
    hyper-personalized advice at scale while generating auditable
    decision trails for compliance (FCA rules on suitability and best
    execution). Advisors could gain a competitive edge by integrating
    these for real-time portfolio adjustments. Yet this **undermines
    traditional banks** at their core. Agentic AI bypasses
    deposit-taking and lending monopolies: autonomous agents could route
    funds directly to non-bank wallets, tokenized assets, or DeFi
    yields, eroding deposit bases and fee income from
    payments/settlement. As AI orchestrates \"money movement\" without
    human or bank intermediation, credit creation shifts toward
    decentralised protocols, pressuring balance sheets and
    profitability. Regulated oversight may mitigate chaos but
    accelerates disintermediation, banks risk becoming mere
    infrastructure providers unless they adopt agentic tech swiftly.
    Does this heralds a future where intelligence, not institutions,
    control the flow of payments, potentially shrinking banks\' economic
    centrality while empowering agile advisors?

2.  **[Why Prime Real Estate Is Becoming Humanity\'s Ultimate
    Asset](https://domosno.com/the-ai-abundance-paradox-why-prime-real-estate-is-becoming-humanitys-ultimate-asset/).**
    As AI pushes the cost of manufacturing, services and even knowledge
    towards zero, the economics of scarcity may shift sharply towards
    assets that technology cannot reproduce. Chief among them: prime
    land. From Alpine resorts to central city districts, the most
    desirable locations remain physically finite even as the global
    supply of goods expands. This helps explain why large investors,
    from technology billionaires to asset managers, have been quietly
    accumulating farmland and trophy real estate. If AI-driven
    productivity generates vast new pools of wealth, demand for scarce
    physical assets could intensify. In such a world, land may
    increasingly function as a store of value, much as art or fine wine
    does today. The implications are already visible in
    ultra-constrained markets such as Monaco or Manhattan, where limited
    supply and global capital have driven prices steadily higher. Yet as
    ownership concentrates, accessibility becomes the next battleground.
    Tokenisation, fractional ownership and co-investment structures are
    emerging as mechanisms to widen participation in otherwise
    unaffordable markets. Digital platforms could allow investors to buy
    slices of properties that would previously have required millions.
    Whether this democratises ownership or merely accelerates capital
    flows into scarce locations remains unclear. But as real estate
    becomes harder to buy outright, the future of property investment
    may increasingly resemble equity markets: divided, tokenised and
    traded in fractions.

3.  **[Bank of Japan to test blockchain-based settlement for central
    bank
    reserves](https://thepaypers.com/crypto-web3-and-cbdc/news/bank-of-japan-to-test-blockchain-settlement-for-central-bank-reserves).**
    This signals that distributed ledger technology is moving closer to
    the core of financial market infrastructure. The pilot will explore
    whether reserves held by commercial banks at the central bank can be
    settled on blockchain networks, potentially enabling faster,
    round-the-clock transfers and reducing the risk of payment gridlock
    during periods of financial stress. While the experiment is still
    exploratory, it reflects a broader shift among central banks toward
    tokenised forms of money and settlement systems. The initiative will
    test how blockchain infrastructure could connect with existing
    systems and support use cases such as interbank payments and
    securities settlement. The move could add pressure on other central
    banks to accelerate similar research. If large economies demonstrate
    that blockchain can improve efficiency, transparency and
    interoperability in core payment infrastructure, the risk of falling
    behind technologically, or fragmenting global settlement networks,
    may push others to follow. Experiments such as wholesale CBDC
    projects and cross-border initiatives already reflect growing
    international competition to modernise financial plumbing.

For policymakers, the strategic question is no longer whether tokenised
settlement will emerge, but who sets the standards. Japan's pilot
therefore highlights a widening race among central banks to shape the
architecture of the next generation of global payments.

4.  **[Banks Want Digital Assets --- Regulators Want the Data to
    Understand
    Them](https://www.finextra.com/the-long-read/1594/digital-assets-are-scaling---data-infrastructure-must-scale-too).**
    Digital assets are entering a new phase of institutionalisation,
    moving beyond experimental pilots into regulated financial
    infrastructure. Tokenised securities, on-chain funds and
    distributed-ledger payment systems are increasingly being issued,
    traded and custodied within traditional financial institutions. As
    this shift accelerates, attention is turning to a less visible but
    critical component of the ecosystem: data infrastructure. The next
    stage of digital asset adoption will depend not only on blockchain
    networks but on globally consistent reference data and
    identification standards that allow institutions to track, report
    and manage tokenised instruments across platforms. For banks, the
    challenge is operational as much as technological. Once digital
    assets appear on regulated balance sheets or within collateral
    frameworks, fragmented identifiers and network-specific conventions
    create risk management and reporting friction. Standardised token
    identification and interoperable data layers will therefore become
    essential to integrate digital assets into liquidity management,
    settlement systems and supervisory reporting. For regulators, the
    shift implies a new supervisory frontier. As digital assets become
    reportable exposures rather than experimental tokens, oversight will
    increasingly depend on data transparency and traceability across
    networks. The question is no longer whether digital assets will
    scale, but whether the underlying data architecture can keep pace.
    If it cannot, the financial system risks building a new market on
    foundations that remain operationally fragmented.

5.  [**UK at the Crossroads: Embrace Stablecoins Wisely, or Watch Rivals
    Rewrite the
    Rules.**](https://www.bankofengland.co.uk/working-paper/2026/a-model-of-monetary-singleness)
    As the Bank of England's 2026 working paper on "A Model of Monetary
    Singleness" warns, stablecoins could fracture the UK's monetary
    unity if encouraged without guardrails. Its theoretical framework
    posits that divergent asset backings in private issuers risk
    "inefficient equilibria," erecting interoperability barriers,
    stifling trade, and eroding welfare through fragmented units of
    account. Systemic perils loom: runs on stablecoins, amplified by
    operational vulnerabilities or overseas exposures, could cascade
    into credit crunches, siphoning bank deposits and hobbling lending,
    echoing ESRB alerts on crypto's spillover threats. A disorderly
    surge might slash real-economy finance, as BoE consultations note,
    with 40% central bank reserves barely cushioning shocks. Yet delays
    in regulation compound dangers. Lagging behind the EU's MiCA and US
    GENIUS Act, the UK invites "digital dollarisation," forcing
    businesses into foreign stablecoins, heightening FX volatility and
    ceding sovereignty. Unchecked, this erodes London's FinTech crown,
    drains talent, and risks isolation as an "island of liquidity." The
    paper urges prudent convergence over mandates, but hesitation
    invites fragility: innovate boldly, or watch rivals rewrite the
    monetary playbook? The City's edge hangs in the balance and
    potentially the \$9+trillion globally of daily FX transactions,
    where the UK has a 38%, market share may no longer favour the UK.

6.  **[AI Agentic models such as Chat GBT favour BTC as a store of value
    and stablecoins for
    payments.](https://blockchain.news/flashnews/ai-agents-favor-bitcoin-for-economic-activity-study-reveals)**
    The rise of agentic AI, autonomous systems capable of making
    decisions and executing transactions, may increase demand for
    digital-native money such as Bitcoin. As AI agents begin to operate
    as independent economic actors, they require payment rails that are
    programmable, borderless, and machine compatible. Blockchain
    networks offer these properties, enabling agents to transact without
    relying on traditional banking infrastructure. Recent research
    suggests this alignment may already be emerging. A study examining
    36 AI models across more than 9,000 responses found that agents
    frequently preferred Bitcoin for economic activity, selecting it in
    48.3% of scenarios, while 79% favoured it as a long-term store of
    value. Stablecoins were more commonly chosen for transactional
    payments, indicating a potential machine-driven digital asset stack
    combining settlement assets and payment tokens. The market for AI
    agents is expanding rapidly, reinforcing the potential scale of this
    shift. Estimates suggest the sector will grow from roughly \$7--8
    billion in 2025 to around \$52 billion by 2030**,** implying growth
    rates of over 40--46% annually**.** If this trajectory continues, an
    "agent economy" could emerge where machines conduct continuous
    micro-transactions, arbitrage, and coordination tasks. In such a
    system, demand for programmable digital assets like Bitcoin may
    rise---not purely as a speculative asset, but as infrastructure
    enabling machine-to-machine economic activity.

7.  [**Why Claude\'s Free Memory Upgrade Could Revolutionise Advisor
    Record-Keeping.**](https://gigazine.net/gsc_news/en/20260303-anthropic-upgrades-claude-memory-attract-ai-switchers/)
    Anthropic's upgrade to Claude\'s memory feature free for all users
    and including easy import from rivals like ChatGPT, marks a pivotal
    step in making AI assistants truly persistent and personalised.
    Claude can now retain key details from past interactions
    (preferences, contexts, settings) across sessions, recalling them
    automatically to inform future responses without repetitive
    prompting. But why does his matters profoundly for legal and
    financial advisors? Regulated companies require immutable, auditable
    records of advice, client instructions, risk assessments, and
    regulatory deliberations. Traditional chat logs are fragmented;
    Claude\'s persistent memory creates a structured, exportable
    knowledge base of interactions---user-controlled, with options to
    pause, delete, or export at any time. Advisors gain:

-   **Seamless continuity**: No re-explaining complex mandates, client
    histories, or compliance frameworks mid-conversation.

-   **Audit-ready trails**: Exported memories serve as verifiable
    timestamps of reasoning and disclosures, bolstering defences under
    FCA, SEC, or MiFID rules.

-   **Efficiency without risk**: Import tools allow secure transfer of
    prior AI contexts (e.g., legacy ChatGPT threads), preserving
    institutional knowledge while switching platforms.

-   **Reduced errors**: Persistent recall minimises oversights in
    ongoing matters like due diligence or KYC updates.

In an era of heightened scrutiny on AI use in regulated professions,
this upgrade transforms Claude from episodic tool to reliable compliance
companion**,** potentially easing record-keeping burdens while raising
the bar for provable diligence. Advisors who adopt it early could gain a
clear edge in accuracy, speed, and defensibility.
