**Weekly Intelligence Brief 26th June 2026**

Artificial intelligence, tokenisation and programmable money are no longer emerging technologies, they are rapidly becoming the operating system of global finance. This week's Intelligence Brief examines how these converging innovations are transforming banking, asset management, payments and legal services, and why the countries, institutions and businesses that adapt first are likely to secure a lasting competitive advantage in the digital economy.

[**Money Without Borders: The Global Financial System Is Being Rebuilt in Real Time**](https://www.iog.io/news/money-without-borders)**.** The vision of "money without borders" is rapidly evolving from fintech ambition into a broader transformation of global finance. As cross-border payment volumes are projected to approach $290 trillion by 2030, financial institutions, central banks and technology firms are racing to modernise the infrastructure that moves money around the world. Traditional correspondent banking networks remain slow, costly and fragmented, creating inefficiencies for businesses, consumers and governments alike. Digital assets, stablecoins, tokenised deposits and programmable payment systems offer the potential for near-instant settlement, lower costs and 24-hour global liquidity. The real opportunity extends beyond faster payments. A more interoperable financial system could reshape trade finance, treasury management, capital markets and machine-to-machine commerce. The strategic question is no longer whether money will move without borders, but which currencies, networks and jurisdictions will control the rails on which future global commerce operates.

[**Tokenisation's ETF Moment: Why AI Could Transform Asset Management Faster Than ETFs Ever Did**](https://www.confexhub.com/2026/06/22/tokenization-mirrors-the-20-trillion-etf-boom-as-blockchain-and-ai-converge-ondo-exec-says/)**.** John Hoffman of Ondo Finance argues that tokenisation today resembles the ETF market in its infancy, before ETFs evolved into a global asset class approaching $20 trillion. However, unlike the ETF revolution, tokenisation is converging with blockchain infrastructure and artificial intelligence at the same time. AI agents could eventually analyse markets, allocate capital and continuously rebalance professionally managed on-chain portfolios in real time, creating an entirely new operating model for investment management. The implications for the asset management industry are profound. Traditional fund managers may need to evolve from manufacturing investment products to providing programmable portfolios, digital custody, tokenised distribution and AI-driven investment intelligence. Those that embrace blockchain infrastructure could lower operating costs, improve liquidity, offer continuous portfolio management and reach global investors more efficiently. The future competitive advantage may depend less on fund selection and more on owning the digital infrastructure through which AI allocates capital.

[**Agentic Payments: The Trillion-Dollar Shift from Human Commerce to Machine Commerce**](https://www.token-relations.com/p/agentic-payments-where-we-are-and-where-we-re-going)**.** Agentic payments represent the convergence of artificial intelligence, digital identity and modern payment infrastructure. Instead of humans manually initiating transactions, AI agents will increasingly manage procurement, subscriptions, treasury operations, travel bookings and digital commerce based on pre-authorised instructions and real-time decision making. The opportunity extends far beyond convenience. Agentic systems could reduce friction, optimise payment routing, automate compliance and improve liquidity management across global markets. However, the rise of machine-to-machine commerce also introduces new challenges around trust, liability, authentication and regulatory oversight. Industry participants are already exploring frameworks that combine AI agents, digital wallets, tokenised assets and programmable payment rails to enable secure autonomous transactions. The strategic implication is profound: payments may evolve from a human-controlled process into an intelligent infrastructure layer where software continuously evaluates, authorises and executes economic decisions on behalf of users and enterprises.

[**The First AI Lawyer Wins in Court: Is This the Beginning of Law's ChatGPT Moment?**](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2026/jun/22/artificial-intelligence-law-firm-wins-court-case-in-england-for-first-time)The legal profession may have reached a historic inflection point. Garfield AI, the UK's first Solicitors Regulation Authority-approved AI law firm, helped a freelance consultant recover £7,000 in unpaid fees after conducting virtually all pre-trial legal work, including correspondence, court filings, witness statements and trial preparation. A human barrister conducted the advocacy, but the case is believed to be the first court victory achieved with the support of a regulated AI law firm.

The significance extends far beyond a small claims dispute. Legal services have traditionally relied on highly trained professionals undertaking time-intensive administrative and research tasks. AI is now beginning to automate many of those functions at a fraction of the cost. The opportunity is greater access to justice and lower legal costs. The challenge is ensuring accuracy, accountability and trust at a time when courts continue to warn about AI-generated errors and hallucinations.

[**Circle and Nomura Redefine FX Settlement: Are Banks Ready for the Stablecoin Era**](https://asia.nikkei.com/spotlight/cryptocurrencies/us-stablecoin-giant-circle-to-start-settlement-business-with-nomura)**.** Circle's reported collaboration with Nomura to introduce near-instant foreign exchange settlement for Japanese corporates using USDC highlights how stablecoins are moving from cryptocurrency markets into mainstream banking infrastructure. The initiative aims to enable businesses to convert yen into digital dollars and complete cross-border settlement within minutes rather than waiting for traditional correspondent banking processes, with a target launch as early as 2027.

For banks, this is strategically important because settlement has historically been a profitable and relationship-driven business. If regulated stablecoins increasingly become the preferred medium for international payments, banks may need to evolve from controlling payment rails to providing digital asset custody, liquidity management, compliance, tokenised deposits and value-added treasury services. Institutions that embrace programmable money could reduce costs, improve capital efficiency and create new revenue streams, while those that delay risk becoming utilities sitting above increasingly digital settlement infrastructure. Japan's regulatory support suggests this transition is accelerating.

[**Tokenised Money Market Funds: The Stablecoin Challenger Banks Didn't See Coming**](https://www.theblock.co/post/406239/trillion-dollar-asset-manager-invesco-plant-flag-tokenized-stablecoin-reserve-sector)**.** Invesco's, a fund manager with over $2.45 trillion of assets under management, decision to launch a tokenised reserve fund reflects the rapid convergence of traditional asset management and blockchain infrastructure. Like BlackRock's BUIDL fund, these products combine the stability of high-quality short-term assets with the efficiency of blockchain-based settlement, allowing investors to move tokenised cash around the clock while earning a market return. This raises an important strategic question for banks and regulators. If tokenised money market funds become widely accepted for payments, they could deliver many of the benefits of stablecoins without being subject to restrictions that prohibit interest payments. Rather than choosing between non-yielding stablecoins and bank deposits, businesses could hold yield-bearing tokenised funds that are instantly transferable and programmable. The result could blur the boundaries between investments, payments and deposits, potentially reshaping treasury management, wholesale payments and the future competitive landscape of digital money.

[**AI Agents and Tokenised Assets: The Next Revolution in Asset Management**](https://blockridge.com/blog/ai-agents-and-rwa-for-asset-management/)**.** The convergence of AI agents and tokenised real-world assets represents one of the most significant structural shifts facing the asset management industry. Tokenisation transforms assets into programmable digital instruments, while AI provides the intelligence to value, monitor, rebalance and optimise portfolios using both on-chain and off-chain data. Together, they could automate compliance, improve liquidity management, personalise investment strategies and enable real-time portfolio optimisation at a scale that has previously been impossible.

For asset managers, this evolution is about far more than reducing operational costs. AI-powered portfolio managers could continuously monitor market conditions, rebalance tokenised portfolios, optimise collateral and execute investment decisions autonomously. Firms that embrace this convergence may evolve into providers of intelligent, programmable investment infrastructure, while those relying on traditional operating models risk losing relevance as digital-first competitors redefine how capital is allocated, managed and distributed.
