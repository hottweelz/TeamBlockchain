**Weekly Intelligence Brief 22nd May 2026**

Finance is quietly being rebuilt beneath our feet. AI agents are starting to hold wallets, stablecoins are becoming programmable money, and identity may become more valuable than payments themselves. The question is no longer whether this transformation happens, but who owns the rails, controls trust and captures the next generation of economic power.

[**Bank of England Softens Stance on Stablecoins: Why English Law + Pragmatic Regulation Could Make the UK the World’s Top Stablecoin Jurisdiction**](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/crypto/articles/bank-england-softens-overly-conservative-131039334.html)**.** The Bank of England is softening its previously strict stance on stablecoins, moving towards a more pragmatic regulatory model that balances innovation with stability. This shift comes after industry pushback and reflects a growing recognition that overly rigid rules could push activity offshore. What makes this particularly significant is English law’s unmatched global influence. English law governs around 40% of international financial contracts, including loans, bonds, derivatives, and cross-border transactions. Its proven flexibility, strong trust principles, and freedom of contract make it ideal for complex programmable money structures. For issuers, launching under English law offers legal certainty, enforceable smart contracts, sophisticated trust and custody mechanisms, and global investor confidence. A more measured BoE approach, allowing realistic reserve compositions and commercial models, could accelerate UK-issued stablecoins in multiple currencies.

The UK now has a genuine opportunity to become the world’s leading jurisdiction for stablecoin innovation, combining regulatory pragmatism with the world’s most respected commercial legal system. The question is whether it will seize this moment before other jurisdictions move faster.

**Business Impact**: Companies that build or integrate agent-compatible stablecoin infrastructure (wallets, compliance, payments) will gain first-mover advantage in the emerging “agent economy.” Enterprises should start experimenting with agentic treasury, automated supplier payments, and programmable commerce now, or risk being left behind as AI agents become major economic participants.

[**The Identity Wars: Why Verifiable Digital Identity May Become the Most Valuable Infrastructure in Finance**](https://www.pymnts.com/digital-identity/2026/why-the-diy-kyc-model-is-facing-a-fraud-reality-check/?utm_source=Sailthru&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=Main%20NL%2005/14&utm_term=Main%20Newsletter)**.** The latest PYMNTS Digital Identity Verification Framework highlights a major structural shift underway in finance: identity verification is evolving from a simple onboarding exercise into a continuous, real-time trust architecture designed for AI-driven commerce and programmable payments. Traditional KYC systems were built for human users performing occasional transactions. That model is breaking down as AI agents increasingly initiate payments, execute trades, manage treasury functions and interact autonomously across digital ecosystems. Financial institutions are now moving toward “identity supply chains” that continuously verify users, devices, counterparties and behavioural signals rather than relying on static credentials alone. At the same time, tokenisation, decentralised identity frameworks and blockchain-based attestations are emerging as critical tools for securing machine-to-machine transactions. The implications are profound. As payments become instant, borderless and autonomous, identity itself may become the new financial infrastructure layer. In a world of programmable money and AI commerce, verifying intent, authenticity and accountability could become more important than the payment itself.

[**The Stablecoin Cold War”: Central Banks Are Starting to Push Back Against the Digital Dollar”.**](https://www.reuters.com/sustainability/boards-policy-regulation/bank-englands-bailey-sees-wrestle-with-us-stablecoin-regulation-2026-05-08/?utm_source=chatgpt.com) Stablecoins are rapidly evolving from crypto trading tools into instruments of geopolitical influence and global monetary competition. While the United States advances stablecoin legislation such as the GENIUS and CLARITY Acts, policymakers at the ECB and Bank of England are increasingly warning that USD-backed stablecoins could weaken domestic currencies, reshape payment systems, and reinforce American financial dominance through “agentic dollars.”

The divide is becoming increasingly visible. The UK and Europe remain cautious, prioritising financial stability, banking regulation, and monetary control. Meanwhile, the US is actively positioning stablecoins as part of its future financial infrastructure strategy. At the same time, adoption across Asia, Africa, and emerging markets continues accelerating because existing banking systems are often slow, fragmented, expensive, or inaccessible. Stablecoins offer near-instant dollar access, low-cost remittances, and mobile-first financial inclusion without requiring traditional banking infrastructure.

The result is a new form of monetary competition where programmable dollars may increasingly circulate outside the traditional banking system itself.
