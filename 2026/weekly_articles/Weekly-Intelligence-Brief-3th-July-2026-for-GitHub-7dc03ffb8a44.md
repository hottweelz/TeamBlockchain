**Weekly Intelligence Brief 4th July 2026**

**We are seeing a fundamental shift in global finance: blockchain is no longer disrupting the financial system, it is becoming the financial system.** As Wall Street embraces tokenised securities, payment giants build programmable money, regulators race to modernise and AI agents prepare to transact autonomously, the strategic question for every board is no longer whether this transformation will happen, but whether their organisation will be positioned to profit from it or compete against it.

[**The $7 Trillion Question: Are Tokenised Money Market Funds About to Replace Corporate Deposit Accounts?**](https://www.marketsmedia.com/180-year-old-new-york-life-adds-to-tokenized-funds/)The 180-year-oldNew York Life Investment Management's decision to launch a tokenised fixed-income fund is significant because it reflects a structural shift rather than another blockchain pilot. With more than $800 billion under management, the firm is joining a growing list of global asset managers embracing blockchain infrastructure to distribute regulated investment products.

For treasury managers, tokenised money market funds could become the next evolution of corporate cash management. Instead of leaving excess liquidity in low-yield deposit accounts, companies could hold programmable, tokenised funds that provide same-day liquidity, generate investment returns, settle instantly and can be used as collateral across digital financial markets. For asset managers, tokenisation opens new global distribution channels while lowering administration and settlement costs. The larger commercial question is whether tomorrow's corporate "deposit account" will increasingly be a tokenised money market fund held in a digital wallet rather than cash sitting idle in a commercial bank. If that transition accelerates, banks will need to compete not only on deposit rates, but on the utility, programmability and interoperability of the money they provide.

[**How Agentic AI Will Reshape Payments**](https://www.imf.org/-/media/files/publications/imf-notes/2026/english/insea2026004.pdf)**.** The IMF's latest note concludes that tokenisation is fundamentally changing the architecture of finance by enabling programmable assets, atomic settlement, continuous liquidity management and embedded compliance on shared digital ledgers. Rather than viewing blockchain as an incremental efficiency gain, the IMF argues it should be seen as a new financial infrastructure capable of reshaping how banks, asset managers, exchanges and payment systems operate. However, the benefits will only be realised if supported by legal certainty, robust governance, trusted settlement assets and international interoperability. Without these foundations, tokenisation could increase fragmentation, concentration risk and financial instability despite improving operational efficiency. For policymakers and financial institutions, the report is a call to action: the competitive advantage will not simply belong to those who tokenise assets, but to jurisdictions that create the legal, regulatory and technological environment in which programmable finance can safely scale.

[**The Real Stablecoin Story: Payments Giants Move From Watching to Building**](https://www.americanbanker.com/payments/news/open-standards-stablecoin-draws-stripe-visa-and-mastercard)**.** A new stablecoin venture backed by more than 140 companies, including Stripe, Visa, Mastercard, Coinbase, BlackRock and BNY, shows how quickly tokenised money is becoming a mainstream payments priority. The project, called Open USD, is designed for global business use cases including cross-border transfers, merchant settlement and payouts, with zero-fee minting and redemption and a shared governance model. Unlike earlier stablecoin efforts dominated by single issuers, this one is framed as a coalition play built around enterprise adoption and infrastructure scale. The broader signal is clear: payments giants are no longer just reacting to stablecoins, they are helping shape the standards that may define them.

[**Robinhood Just Fired the Starting Gun: Are Traditional Stock Exchanges Ready for the On-Chain Era?**](https://thedefiant.io/news/blockchains/robinhood-launches-robinhood-chain-mainnet-adds-stock-tokens-onchain-lending-and-agentic-crypto) Robinhood's launch represents far more than another blockchain initiative. By combining its Layer-2 blockchain with tokenised equities, decentralised lending, stablecoin yield products and AI-powered trading agents, the company is creating an integrated financial ecosystem where investing, settlement and collateral management occur natively on-chain. Tokenised stocks are now available through Robinhood Wallet in more than 120 jurisdictions, dramatically expanding global access to U.S. equities while enabling 24/7 trading and programmable financial services.

For traditional stock exchanges, this is a strategic warning. Exchanges such as the New York Stock Exchange, Nasdaq and the London Stock Exchange have historically generated value by acting as centralised venues for listing, trading and settlement. On-chain markets compress these functions into programmable infrastructure where issuance, trading, clearing, settlement and even securities lending can occur almost instantly on a single network. If tokenised securities continue expanding internationally, traditional exchanges risk evolving from being the marketplace itself to becoming trusted issuers and regulatory gateways connected to blockchain networks. The commercial winners will be those exchanges that embrace tokenisation, AI-driven trading and 24/7 markets before new entrants redefine global capital markets around blockchain rather than trading floors.

[**Why the UK’s Stablecoin Regime Matters for Banks, Businesses and Digital Money**](https://www.tradefinanceglobal.com/posts/bank-of-england-and-fca-publish-new-framework-shaping-uk-stablecoin-regulation/)**.** The Bank of England and FCA have published a new stablecoin framework that gives the UK a clearer path toward regulated sterling stablecoins, with final rules expected later in 2026 and implementation from 2027. The regime is designed to support safe innovation while allowing stablecoins to be used for faster, cheaper and more flexible payments, including cross-border use cases and programmable transactions. Commercially, the biggest implication is for bank deposits and credit creation: the Bank is trying to avoid excessive outflows from the banking system by using temporary issuance guardrails and reserve rules, while still making stablecoin models commercially viable. For businesses, the upside is quicker settlement, better working-capital efficiency and lower payment friction.

[**Wall Street Goes On-Chain: Securitize's NYSE Debut Marks the Start of the Tokenised Securities Era**](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/articles/securitize-begins-trading-nyse-tokenized-184350320.html). By making its shares available on-chain, Securitize demonstrates how equities can trade with faster settlement, improved transparency and potentially 24-hour availability. Asset managers, exchanges, custodians and transfer agents now face increasing pressure to modernise legacy infrastructure or risk becoming less competitive. Tokenisation also creates opportunities for programmable compliance, automated corporate actions and fractional ownership. For businesses, this is another indicator that blockchain's commercial future lies in digitising traditional financial assets rather than creating entirely new ones. The tokenisation race is increasingly becoming a contest over financial market infrastructure.
