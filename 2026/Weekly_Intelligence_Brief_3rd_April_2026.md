# Weekly Intelligence Brief – 3rd April 2026

In this week’s intelligence brief, the future of money moves from theory to execution. Larry Fink declares tokenisation will remake capital markets as profoundly as the internet transformed information. Central banks, led by the RBA’s Project Acacia, are no longer debating digital money, they are designing it. Major banks like MUFG are adopting JPMorgan’s Kinexys blockchain platform for real-world global transfers, while stablecoins gain mainstream traction in high-growth economies. At the edges, stablecoins are scaling fastest, driven by younger, high-growth markets where utility is outrunning regulation.

The message is clear: 2026 is the year programmable, tokenised finance shifts from innovation to infrastructure.

## **[Larry Fink Declares Tokenisation Will Remake Capital Markets Like the Internet Remade Information](https://www.blackrock.com/corporate/investor-relations/larry-fink-annual-chairmans-letter)**

In his 2026 Annual Chairman’s Letter, BlackRock CEO Larry Fink positions tokenisation as the next transformative leap in global finance, comparable to the internet in the 1990s. He argues that with half the world’s population already carrying digital wallets on their phones, tokenisation can fundamentally update the “plumbing” of capital markets by making it dramatically easier to issue, trade, settle, and access investments.
