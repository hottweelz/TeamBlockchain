# Digital Bytes

## Weekly Intelligence Brief — 13 March 2026

**AI Payments, Tokenised Markets and the Rewiring of Financial Infrastructure**

A defining signal this week came from **Visa**, which stated that **every bank should have a stablecoin strategy by 2025.**

This is not merely a payments innovation narrative. It reflects a deeper structural transition in global finance, from batch-based, human-initiated financial systems toward **programmable, autonomous and continuously operating capital markets.**

Across multiple developments this week, one theme is clear: **Money, markets and decision-making are becoming machine-native infrastructure.**

### 1. AI becomes core payments infrastructure

[NVIDIA’s emerging vision for AI in payments](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/georgios-kolovos-inside-nvidias-vision-ai-payments-sam-boboev-js7ie/) **NVIDIA is articulating a vision in which payments evolve from transaction rails into intelligence engines.**

Large AI models trained on transaction datasets may simultaneously perform fraud detection, credit underwriting, behavioural prediction and personalisation.

This signals a shift toward **“payment foundation models”** capable of managing financial decisions in real time.

The logical extension is agentic commerce: AI systems initiating and executing payments autonomously within defined mandates.

For banks and wealth advisers, competitive advantage may increasingly depend on proprietary data models rather than product distribution.

### 2. Inclusion, tokenisation and the redesign of capital markets

[The Digital Economy’s Next Chapter Must Be About Inclusion.](https://thefintechtimes.com/the-digital-economys-next-chapter-must-be-about-inclusion/) The next phase of digital transformation is likely to focus on participation rather than connectivity alone.

Tokenisation allows real-world assets such as bonds, funds and real estate to be represented as programmable digital instruments. This lowers investment thresholds, enhances liquidity and expands global investor access.

Financial advice is therefore evolving. Advisers may increasingly guide clients through **digital custody models, tokenised portfolio construction and infrastructure risk**, rather than traditional fund selection alone.

### 3. Exchanges are becoming digital infrastructure providers

[Deutsche Börse and Allfunds: Infrastructure for Tokenised Investment Funds and Digital Capital Markets](https://www.reuters.com/business/deutsche-boerse-reaches-deal-acquire-allfunds-2026-01-21/) The proposed acquisition of **Allfunds** by **Deutsche Börse** highlights a structural shift in market architecture.

By integrating distribution networks with custody, settlement and data services, exchanges are positioning themselves as **platform builders for tokenised securities.**

Tokenised funds require issuance, investor access and programmable settlement.  
Combining these capabilities could enable near-real-time fund dealing, fractional ownership and cross-border distribution at scale.

### 4. AI search reshapes marketing economics

[AI Is Stealing Your Traffic- Your Google Ranking Means Nothing](https://explodingtopics.com/blog/ai-search-optimization-guide) Large language models such as **ChatGPT** and **Perplexity AI** are changing how information is discovered.

Instead of ranking web pages, AI systems synthesise answers. Visibility increasingly depends on **being cited as a trusted source rather than appearing first in search results.**

This transition is driving the rise of **Generative Engine Optimisation (GEO)**, structuring content so machines can interpret, reference and reuse it.

### 5. Blockchain enters the liquidity core of financial markets

[Blockchain as Repo Market Infrastructure: $7.3 Trillion Transactions in a month Signals Institutional Adoption](https://simplywall.st/stocks/us/commercial-services/nyse-br/broadridge-financial-solutions/news/is-broadridge-br-quietly-redefining-its-growth-story-with-bl) Institutional adoption of distributed ledger technology is becoming visible in repo markets, one of the primary funding mechanisms of global finance.

Platforms processing trillions in collateralised transactions demonstrate that blockchain is evolving into **market plumbing rather than speculative infrastructure.**

Faster settlement enhances collateral mobility, reduces counterparty risk and improves liquidity transmission across bond, derivatives and equity markets.

### 6. CFOs move from AI analysis to AI execution

[CFOs Are Learning to Trust AI — Next Comes the Payments](https://www.pymnts.com/study_posts/what-happens-when-cfos-get-serious-about-gen-ai/). Corporate finance leaders are embedding AI into capital allocation, liquidity modelling and risk oversight.

The next phase involves supervised autonomy: AI systems authorised to trigger payments, rebalance working capital or optimise supplier financing.

This could redefine financial governance, shifting from approval hierarchies toward algorithmic execution frameworks.

### 7. Machine-to-machine payments reach live deployment

[AI Agents execute EU 1st First Real Payment using Mastercard and Santander](https://www.santander.com/en/press-room/press-releases/2026/03/santander-and-mastercard-complete-europes-first-live-end-to-end-payment-executed-by-an-ai-agent?utm_source=chatgpt.com) A demonstration involving **Banco Santander** and **Mastercard** showed an AI agent completing an end-to-end payment on real banking rails.

This marks a transition from automated finance to **autonomous finance.**

As digital assistants increasingly transact on behalf of users and firms, payment networks may become embedded into software workflows rather than initiated manually.

Banks that fail to support programmable transaction layers risk becoming balance-sheet utilities while platforms control customer relationships.

## Digital Bytes Macro View

Taken together, these developments suggest that financial innovation is no longer centred on individual technologies such as blockchain or artificial intelligence.

Instead, the financial system is undergoing **architectural convergence.**

- AI is becoming the decision engine
- Tokenisation is becoming the asset layer
- Stablecoins and programmable deposits are becoming the settlement medium
- Exchanges and banks are becoming infrastructure orchestrators

The result is the emergence of **continuous, data-driven and machine-executable financial markets.**

The institutions that align strategy across these layers are likely to define the next era of global finance.

Those that treat them as separate trends risk structural obsolescence.
